How to Create a Bindrune for Wealth Protection: Fehu, Algiz & Othala Combination

By Frater Alek0s • June 24, 2026 • 10 min read

What Is a Bindrune and Why Use It for Wealth Protection?

A bindrune is a composite symbol created by overlaying two or more Elder Futhark runes into a single glyph. Unlike casting runes for divination, a bindrune functions as a continuous magical sigil — it carries the combined energy of its constituent runes toward a specific intention.

For wealth protection, we combine three complementary runes: Fehu (ᚠ) for financial abundance, Algiz (ᛉ) for protective defense, and Othala (ᛟ) for ancestral grounding and property security. Together, they create a bindrune that attracts wealth while shielding it from loss.

Bindrune for Wealth Protection: Fehu Meaning

Fehu (ᚠ) — The first rune of the Elder Futhark, associated with cattle, mobile wealth, and abundance. Fehu represents not just money but movable assets: income flow, investments, and liquid resources. Its energy is active, generative, and opportunistic.

Bindrune for Wealth Protection: Algiz Meaning

Algiz (ᛉ) — The elk rune, representing protection, spiritual defense, and higher consciousness. Algiz acts as a shield around your wealth, deflecting financial attacks, theft, envy, and poor decisions. It also connects your intention to divine protection.

Bindrune for Wealth Protection: Othala Meaning

Othala (ᛟ) — The ancestral property rune, representing inheritance, homeland, and long-term security. Othala grounds the bindrune in physical reality, anchoring wealth to your family line and physical assets like property and land.

How to Draw the Fehu Algiz Othala Bindrune

  1. Start with Othala (ᛟ) as the foundation — draw its diamond-like shape with angled feet
  2. Overlay Fehu (ᚠ) along the central axis — its angled branches rising through the Othala diamond
  3. Cross with Algiz (ᛉ) — its upward-reaching arms extending beyond the other runes to form a protective canopy
  4. Merge shared lines — where Fehu branches overlap Algiz arms, draw a single continuous line

The result is a compact, balanced glyph that encodes all three runes in a single visual statement.

Q&A: Bindrune Creation for Wealth Protection

Can I add more runes to this bindrune?

Yes, but with caution. Each additional rune dilutes the specific focus. For wealth protection, three runes is optimal. Adding Ansuz (communication) or Raidho (journey) shifts the intention toward business travel or negotiations rather than pure protection.

What material should I carve the bindrune on?

Wood (oak for strength, ash for protection), metal (silver for wealth affinity), or parchment. Digital bindrunes rendered on screen are equally effective — the medium matters less than the charging. Our Norse Rune Oracle app can generate this bindrune digitally.

How often should I recharge the bindrune?

Monthly, ideally during the new moon or on a Thursday (Jupiter's day for expansion). Carry it as a talisman or display it in your workspace. Recharging reinforces the energetic imprint.
